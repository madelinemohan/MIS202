Learning Objectives

This assignment will cover a variety of ways to use GIS application to work with location data. You are encouraged to explore on your own to find additional features that will make your life and present or future jobs easier. Men will benefit once they understand that thanks to GPS devices, they will never have to ask for directions again.

After completing this assignment you will be able to use a GIS application to

  • find a location on the earth given a latitude and longitude
  • find the latitude and longitude of a specific location on the earth
  • define geographic territories and layers
  • validate street addresses
  • work with demographic to determine a business site location
  • develop an appreciation for spatial awareness
  • combine numerical or textual and spatial data in a mashup

The exercises in this assignment should be done in the order presented.
For a background on the history of navigation and time, check out this article from the Smithstonian: Getting From Here to There

If you enjoy working with geographic information systems, be aware that you can pursue a Master's Degree in GIS at St. Mary's University of Winona. Several alumni have done this.

Assignment Overview

The purpose of this assignment is to learn how businesses and other entities can benefit from an understanding of GIS and GIS software. Most cell phones are becoming location aware. Learn how this can allow a business to improve existing services and offer new ones. Some other things to consider are:

  • What are latitude and longitude and why should I care?
  • Does my business already have location information that it is not using?
  • What opportunities are there to leverage our location data?
  • What can GIS software do for our business?
  • How can I keep ahead of my customers who are GIS savvy?
